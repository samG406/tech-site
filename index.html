How Sylvan Works

Structure / Series / Issuance Layers

Structure: Never changes. Rules, waterfall, and governance framework.

Series: Timing only. Tax year and distribution schedule.

Issuance: Assets and metrics. Specific properties, capital stack, and coverage ratios.

Global Invariants

Leverage is fixed at close.

No mezzanine debt, incremental borrowing, or leverage-increasing refinances are permitted.

Assets do not change post-close; if assets change, a new issuance is created.

Distributions adjust to performance; leverage does not.

Capital Position

Sylvan capital sits senior to sponsor equity and junior to bank debt. Sponsor equity absorbs first loss.

Income Waterfall

1
Rent collected
2
Operating expenses
3
Required operating and debt service reserves
4
Senior debt service
5
Net distributable cash to Sylvan investors

Income & Downside Behavior

Reserves are utilized before distributions adjust and are replenished pursuant to defined priority rules before distributions normalize.

In stress scenarios, reserve usage precedes any distribution adjustment. Once performance stabilizes, reserves are restored before distributions return to target levels.

Offerings

Shared Characteristics

Governance & Exam Narrative

CIO Framework

Per-issuance review: Evaluate specific assets, capital stack, and coverage metrics for each new issuance.

Portfolio limits: Establish allocation boundaries and concentration limits appropriate to client profiles.

CCO Framework

Version-controlled materials: All offering documents, disclosures, and client communications maintain version control.

Material-change triggers: Clear definition of what constitutes a material change requiring disclosure update.

Audit-ready logs: Complete record of approvals, allocations, and client communications.

Fail-Fast Guardrail

If a deal needs special rules, it does not belong on Sylvan.

This ensures structural consistency and prevents governance exceptions that undermine repeatability.

Portfolio Role & Comparisons

Why Not REITs

REITs provide liquidity and diversification but lack asset-level transparency and control over leverage decisions. Sylvan offers fixed leverage, known assets, and no daily mark-to-market volatility.

How Sylvan Complements Bonds

Real estate income with fixed leverage provides different risk characteristics than corporate or government bonds. Sylvan offers real asset collateral, inflation sensitivity, and income not correlated to interest rate duration.

Rebalancing Through Flows

Portfolio adjustments occur through new allocations and natural exits, not secondary trading. This matches the liquidity profile of the underlying real estate assets.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this a fund?

No. Sylvan is an issuance-based structure. Each issuance has defined assets that do not change post-close.

Who makes decisions?

RIAs make allocation decisions for their clients. Sylvan provides the structure and documentation framework. Property-level decisions are made by the property manager within defined operating parameters.

Can leverage change?

No. Leverage is fixed at close. No mezzanine debt, incremental borrowing, or leverage-increasing refinances are permitted.

What happens in stress?

Reserves are utilized before distributions adjust and are replenished pursuant to defined priority rules before distributions normalize. Sponsor equity absorbs first loss.

Taxable vs tax-advantaged accounts?

Core Income and Offset can be held in either account type. Offset provides depreciation timing benefits most relevant in taxable accounts. DST Access is designed for 1031 exchange transactions.

Who is this not for?

Sylvan is not appropriate for investors requiring liquidity, daily pricing, or the ability to redeem. This is a closed-end structure with exit via property sale or refinance.

How do I explain this to clients?

"This is structured real estate income with fixed rules and changing properties. The leverage, waterfall, and governance stay the same across opportunities. You approve the structure once, then review individual real estate assets as they become available. It's custodian-held, closed-end, and designed to complement your existing fixed income allocation."

Classification: Sylvan is not an issuer, investment adviser, broker-dealer, or custodian. Sylvan provides standardized structure, workflow, and audit-ready documentation used by RIAs and their clients.
